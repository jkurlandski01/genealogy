Katie Kurlanski

On this page I hope one day to tell the story of Katherine "Katie" Kurlanski, my great-great aunt and first-born child of my great-great grandparents, Sophia and Louis Kurlandski.

Right now the story consists of just two census records and a passing mention made by Rene, my Great-Uncle Frank's daughter. But with a little digging perhaps I will learn more.

I call her "Katie Kurlanski" because that's how she appears in the 1910 U.S. Census, where I first learned of her existence.

source: 1910 Census for Ludwik Kurlanski

In the census ten years later she is recorded as "Catherine Kurlandski," age 13.

Katherine in the 1920 Census

There is no listing of her in the family's record in the 1930 census. Since there was never any mention of her in the family's oral history either, I immediately concluded she died sometime after 1920.

The Family Record in the 1930 Census

However, just recently Rene has sent an email mentioning a daughter that her grandfather, i.e. Louis/Ludwig Kurlandski, disowned because she married a Mason. Rene says her father, Frank, found Katie when Katie's husband passed away. Rene even has a vague memory of at least one time visiting her black sheep of an aunt.

So far, that's all we have.
